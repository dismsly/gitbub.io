It Started with a Recurring Moment of Madness.

What looked like just pickup revealed a systemic failure. Every day, arrivals, dismissals, and bus transitions waste millions of parent hours, frustrate staff, and expose safety gaps. In a world of ongoing safety threats, not knowing when and where children are is no longer acceptable.

A System in Sync: A View for Everyone

Dismisly works like modern infrastructure that connects parents, staff, and students in real time. No shouting, no confusion, no wasted minutes.

The Parent

Before: Anxiety about readiness, line position, and time.

With Dismisly: Peace of mind. From drop off to the ride home, you know when your child boarded, where they are headed, and if something did not go as planned.

The Staff

Before: Chaos with car lines, buses, and last minute changes using paper lists and walkie talkies.

With Dismisly: Calm control. A real time view shows who is here, who has boarded, and who still needs to be accounted for. You manage the flow, not the chaos.

The Student

Before: Confusion in crowded halls and missed announcements.

With Dismisly: Simple clarity. Clear instructions tell you where to go such as Lane 3, Bus A, or After School Room. You move confidently, safely, and on time.

A Note from Our Founder

Melissa Zellner Gomez

My mission to create Dismisly began with a recurring moment of madness: waiting in a disorganized school pickup line, knowing that the inefficiency was not just frustrating, but unsafe. That personal frustration sparked a journey of discovery. What looked like just a dismissal problem revealed a systemic failure. I saw a landscape of fragmented apps that failed to gain national adoption because they did not address the full, complex reality of how students move through their day.

This investigation transformed my project into a mission. As a parent, I saw the massive, unaddressed safety gaps. As a systems strategist who has spent two decades translating chaos into coordination for government and philanthropic clients, I saw the operational inefficiencies. And as a former first-generation student in NYC who navigated the city alone, I understood the vulnerability of those unmonitored journeys intimately.

Dismisly was born from that collision of experience and care. What began as a solution for dismissal has expanded into a vision to modernize every system that moves children between home, school, and beyond. My background in Information Technology provided the foundation to architect the solution, and my professional experience managing complex, equity-focused initiatives has prepared me to execute the plan.

This is a journey of discovery into a rigorously tested, evidence-based platform that can deliver on a simple, powerful promise: Know Where. Know When. No Stress.
